# Client Onboarding Template — One Person Company

> A repeatable onboarding flow so every new client starts smoothly — without you reinventing the wheel each time. Copy this into [Notion](https://www.notion.so/) (or any docs tool) and reuse it for every engagement.

**Source & more templates:** [onepersoncompany.com](https://onepersoncompany.com/)

## How to use this template

1. Duplicate this page for each new client (rename it `Onboarding — [Client Name]`).
2. Work top to bottom. Each checkbox is a step you (or an automation) complete.
3. Keep the master copy clean — only ever edit the duplicates.

## 2. Pre-kickoff checklist

- [ ] Proposal accepted / contract signed
- [ ] Deposit or first invoice paid
- [ ] Shared folder created and access granted
- [ ] Client added to project tracker
- [ ] Access to required accounts/tools requested
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled

## 3. Kickoff call agenda (30–45 min)

1. Introductions & working style (5 min)
2. Goals and definition of success (10 min)
3. Scope, milestones, and timeline (10 min)
4. Communication cadence & response times (5 min)
5. Risks, dependencies, and what you need from them (5 min)
6. Next steps & owner for each (5 min)

## 4. Expectations one-pager (send after kickoff)

- **Scope:** what is and is not included.
- **Timeline:** key milestones with dates.
- **Communication:** channel, hours, and expected response time.
- **Revisions:** how many rounds are included.
- **Payment:** schedule and method.
- **Out of scope:** how new requests are quoted.

## 5. First-week deliverables

- [ ] Shared the expectations one-pager
- [ ] Delivered first small win (builds trust early)
- [ ] Confirmed access to everything needed
- [ ] Logged the project in your weekly review

## 6. Recurring touchpoints

| Cadence | Touchpoint |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Progress update (async or call) |
| Milestone | Review + sign-off |
| Monthly | Invoice + relationship check-in |

## Why this matters for a one-person company

When you are the whole team, every minute of re-figuring-out a process is lost revenue. A standard onboarding flow means new clients feel a polished, professional experience while you spend zero willpower remembering what to do next.
