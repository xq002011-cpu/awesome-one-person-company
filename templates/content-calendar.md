# Content Calendar Template — One Person Company

> A simple system for planning, batching, and shipping content consistently as a team of one. Copy this into [Notion](https://www.notion.so/) and use it as a database (table → board → calendar views).

**Source & more templates:** [onepersoncompany.com](https://onepersoncompany.com/)

---

## How to use this template

1. Recreate the table below as a Notion **database**.
2. Add three views over the same data: **Table** (backlog), **Board** (by Status), **Calendar** (by Publish date).
3. Batch in two passes: one session to plan/outline, one session to write/produce. Never plan and create in the same sitting.

## Weekly content cadence (example for a solo founder)

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Monday | Pull 3 ideas into the backlog |
| Tuesday | Outline the week's main piece |
| Wednesday | Write / record (batch) |
| Thursday | Edit + schedule |
| Friday | Repurpose 1 piece into 3 short ones |

## The 1 → 3 repurposing rule

Every long piece (blog post, video, newsletter) should produce **at least 3** short derivatives:

- [ ] A LinkedIn post
- [ ] An X/Twitter thread or single post
- [ ] A short-form video or carousel

## Content pillars (define yours)

1. **Pillar 1 —** (e.g. teach your craft)
2. **Pillar 2 —** (e.g. share your journey)
3. **Pillar 3 —** (e.g. results & proof)

Aim for roughly 80% value, 20% promotion.

---

## Why this matters for a one-person company

Consistency beats intensity. The solo founders who win on content are not the most talented writers — they're the ones who shipped every week for a year. A calendar removes the daily "what should I post?" decision and lets you batch, so content fits around the rest of the business instead of eating it.
